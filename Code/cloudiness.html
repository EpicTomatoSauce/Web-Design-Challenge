Cloudiness


There is not necessarily any trend between city latitudes and cloudiness levels. We can't say cloudiness increase or decrease when latitude get closer to the equator. However, it would appear that there may be slightly higher concentration of cities which experience more cloud between 40-60 degrees north of the equator relative to other latitude segments. As noted by Geerts & Linacre, continents tend to be less cloudy than adjacent oceans. As there is a greater land mass above the equator, this could explain the aforementioned observation. However, the data provided may be limited to on land observations, and may not properly represent at-sea cloudiness at each latitude.
